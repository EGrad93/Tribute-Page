Jackie Robinson

The athlete that broke the color barrier in sports
On April 15, 1947, Jackie Robinson became the first black athlete to play in Major League Baseball (and any major American sport) when he played in a game for the Brooklyn Dodgers.

    A timeline of Jackie Robinson's life:

  • 1919 - Born in Cairo, Georgia
  • 1920 - Family moved to Passedena, CA
  • 1937 - Enrolled in Passedena Junior College
  • 1939 - Enrolled in UCLA
  • 1941 - Played professional football for the Honolulu Bears
  • 1942 - Drafted into Army
  • 1944 - Honorably discharged from Army
  • 1945 - Resumed baseball career with Negro League team Kansas City Monarchs
  • 1946 - Married Rachel Isum, a nursing student he while at UCLA
  • 1947 - Drafted to MLB team Brooklyn Dodgers
  • 1949 - Named Most Valuable Player for National League
  • 1956 - Retires from baseball after being drafted to the N.Y. Giants
  • 1962 - Inducted into Baseball Hall of Fame
  • 1972 - Passed away at 53 from a heart attack

“To do what he did has got to be the most tremendous thing I’ve ever seen in sports.”

-- Pee Wee Reese, Hall of Fame Dodger shortstop and teammate of Jackie Robinson
